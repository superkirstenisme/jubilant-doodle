Why Coaching?

Welcome. I'm Akwe Amosu, a developmental coach with experience as a leader and senior manager with responsibility for organizational culture and health. I've worked as a human rights advocate, and as a journalist and editor. I'm also a poet. I am certified as a Professional Coach with Integral Coaching Canada and work with clients from a wide range of backgrounds. The rigor of the Integral methodology offers a strong framework for a client's growth yet puts high value on intuition, curiosity and enquiry, allowing both coach and client to feel partnership, trust and agency as they work together. A coaching program is usually six to eight sessions, tailored to the individual, and can be extended if desired.

I come to coaching with a belief that self-awareness, curiosity and the courage to grow are vital assets, even when they bring discomfort. That's true in personal or community spaces, and it is also key in the workplace. Living and working well alongside others requires constant learning and adaptation. This is particularly the case for leaders who need to build trust with their teams. Coaching can help deepen and expand these capacities.

Sometimes my clients have a specific issue — perhaps a work relationship that keeps going off the rails, or anxiety about taking a new role. Perhaps there's a painful disappointment to process, or grief that feels stuck. Alternatively, they're ready to make a positive shift in their life but something keeps getting in the way. Or they just feel stale — a life that used to be satisfying no longer feels good. Whatever the reason, working through it with a thoughtful coach can be helpful.

When you choose coaching, you are attending to your own needs — but you will be doing something else that is important. Whether at home, in our neighborhood or workplace, in our activism or our volunteering, we are always in community. When we feel positive and aligned with our own sense of meaning and purpose, we help those around us to thrive. We are each one tree, but together we make a forest. The value of coaching extends beyond our personal growth.

We are in turbulent times that may trigger anxiety about the future amid rising unpredictability. Even those energized by disruption and the opportunities it can bring may feel uneasy at the constant churn. At such a moment, it can be difficult to stay connected to a sense of purpose, to stay open and curious as we try to do good work, and care for ourselves and others. But with reliable support, and confidence to pursue what we deeply care about, we can regain balance, take pleasure in the world despite the headwinds, and keep learning.

I aim to provide that warm and engaged support to my clients — in service to them and, by extension, their community. For each tree, and for the forest.

About Me

I worked for over two decades as a journalist and editor (West Africa magazine, the Financial Times, the BBC World Service and allAfrica.com). After a short spell with the United Nations, I switched to being a full-time advocate for human rights and social justice with the Open Society Foundations and later joined senior management there. In all my roles, I've seen how hard it is to achieve good results when both confidence and trust are low. I've also seen that when one individual makes a positive change, it can trigger growth and new energy in the whole team.

Most recently I led a five-year project bringing nearly 200 human rights leaders together from 70 countries for immersive conversations about how we might strengthen organizations and movements that defend rights and increase their power and impact. The participants taught me a great deal about leading with generosity and wisdom, and what it takes to translate good intentions into positive outcomes.

And in the background, I have always been a writer, mostly of poetry. My book, Not Goodbye, was published in 2010 and my poems have appeared in US and South African journals — The Common, Illuminations, Carapace, New Contrast, and others.
